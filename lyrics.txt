The COVID-19 pandemic, also known as the 
coronavirus pandemic, is an ongoing global pandemic of 
coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) caused by severe acute 
respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). The novel
 virus was first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019;
   to contain the outbreak, and it spread to other parts of mainland 
   China and around the world. The World Health Organization (WHO)
    declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on 30 January 2020, and a pandemic on 11 March 2020. Since 2021, variants of the virus have emerged and become dominant in many countries, with the Delta, Alpha and Beta variants being the most virulent. As of 3 October 2021, more than 234 million cases and 4.8 million deaths have been confirmed, making it one of the deadliest pandemics in history.
